PRISHTINA INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT

The renovation of Prishtina Airport in 2013 was a significant project aimed at improving the facilities and infrastructure of the airport to meet the growing demands of air travel in the region. The renovation project included various aspects such as expanding and modernizing the terminal building, upgrading the runway, taxiways, and apron, as well as enhancing the airport's overall operational efficiency and safety. One of the main goals of the renovation was to increase the airport's capacity to handle a larger number of passengers and aircraft.

KITKA WIND FARM

The Kitka wind farm, located in Kamenica, Kosovo, is a facility that generates renewable energy through the use of wind turbines. Wind turbines are large structures that convert wind energy into electrical energy. To set up these turbines, special transport and assembly procedures are necessary. Transportation of wind turbines typically involves moving various components, such as turbine blades, tower sections, and nacelles (the housing for the generator and other equipment), from the manufacturing site to the installation location. This process requires specialized vehicles, such as flatbed trucks or trailers that can carry oversized and heavy loads.
